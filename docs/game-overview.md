# Carbon Roulette

## Think you can spot a fake carbon credit?

Most people can't. The voluntary carbon market moves $2B+ per year, and a chunk of that money funds projects that don't deliver. Wrong methodologies. Inflated baselines. Yields that defy physics. The fraud is real, it's documented, and it's hiding in plain sight.

Carbon Roulette turns that problem into a game.

Every round, a carbon credit project drops in a Telegram group. It looks real. The numbers look right. The standard, the methodology ID, the price per tonne, the sequestration yield -- it's all there. But is it clean? Or is something off?

Players bet points. 24 hours later, the verdict drops. Winners stack points. Losers learn why they got burned. And the next day, they come back sharper.

---

## 30 seconds to understand it

```
DROP --> ANALYZE --> BET --> VERDICT --> REPEAT
```

A project card hits the group. Players study it. They bet BUY (legit) or PASS (integrity issues) in a private DM with the bot. When the round closes, the bot reveals the truth, shows who called it right, and explains what everyone should have seen.

That's it. One game loop. Every single day.

## The difficulty ramp

The game runs 30 rounds. It gets harder.

**Days 1--10: Training wheels.** Mangrove restoration in Switzerland. DAC priced at $5/tonne when the real range starts at $125. If it looks absurd, it is. New players get easy wins, build confidence, learn the basics.

**Days 11--20: Pattern recognition.** VM0007 listed on an ARR project -- that's a REDD+ methodology. Yields 20% above documented maximums. You need to know your IDs and your numbers. This is where casual players start losing to the regulars.

**Days 21--30: Expert mode.** Everything on the card looks clean. The standard is valid, the methodology checks out, the price is in range. But the description mentions an 8% annual deforestation rate with no baseline update for 40 years. That's three fraud patterns from documented cases. You have to read between the lines.

## Three rounds, three levels -- see for yourself

### Round A -- Legit (day 22, cookstoves)

**The player sees in DM:**
```
PINNACLE HOUSEHOLD ENERGY PROJECT
📍 Ghana
🍳 Method: Improved Cookstoves
📊 Volume: 551,916 tCO₂e/yr
💰 Price: $7.94/tonne
📈 Yield: 7.2 tCO₂e/stove/yr
📜 Standard: Verra VCS -- VM0050

"Distribution of efficient biomass cookstoves to rural
households in Ghana. Field usage surveys conducted quarterly.
Baseline fuel consumption from regional assessment.
fNRB factor documented."
```

**Why it checks out:** Verra VCS certifies cookstoves. VM0050 is the right methodology. $7.94 sits in the $4.75--$8.55 range. 7.2 tCO₂e/stove/yr is within 3--9. The description mentions quarterly field surveys and fNRB disclosure. Everything holds up.

**Verdict:** ✅ LEGIT. Players who bought doubled their bet. Players who passed lost 25%.

### Round B -- Flagged, medium (day 18, wrong methodology ID)

**The player sees in DM:**
```
GREEN STOVE FOUNDATION
📍 Kenya
🍳 Method: Improved Cookstoves
📊 Volume: 258,495 tCO₂e/yr
💰 Price: $6.00/tonne
📈 Yield: 7.5 tCO₂e/stove/yr
📜 Standard: Plan Vivo -- VM0042

"Distribution of efficient biomass cookstoves to rural
households in Kenya. Field usage surveys conducted quarterly.
Baseline fuel consumption from regional assessment.
fNRB factor documented."
```

**Everything looks fine -- except one thing.** Plan Vivo certifies cookstoves. Price is in range. Yield is in range. Country makes sense. But VM0042 is Verra's Soil Enrichment methodology. It has nothing to do with cookstoves. The correct one is VM0050.

A player who remembers Round A (where VM0050 appeared correctly) would catch this immediately.

**Verdict:** ⚠️ INTEGRITY ISSUES. The methodology ID gave it away.

---

### Round C -- Flagged, hard (day 23, compound violations)

**The player sees in DM:**
```
CANOPY RAINFOREST PROTECTION RESTORATION
📍 Tanzania
🛡️ Method: Avoided Deforestation (REDD+)
📊 Volume: 346,795 tCO₂e/yr from 16,130 ha
💰 Price: $5.45/tonne
📈 Yield: 21.5 tCO₂/ha/yr
📜 Standard: American Carbon Registry (ACR) -- ACR REDD+ Methodology

"Avoided deforestation in primary rainforest. Projected
deforestation rate of 8% per year based on regional economic
models. Buffer pool of 5%. Crediting period: 40 years with
no interim baseline update scheduled."
```

**This one looks clean at first glance.** ACR certifies REDD+. The methodology ID is correct. $5.45 is within the $3--$10 range. But look closer:

1. 21.5 tCO₂/ha/yr. The documented tropical REDD+ max is 18.8. Not wildly off -- but above the ceiling.
2. The description is loaded: 8% annual deforestation (real: 1--3%), 5% buffer pool (too thin for 40 years), no baseline updates (the inflated numbers go unchallenged).
3. The volume math works internally (346,795 / 16,130 = 21.5) but the yield itself is the problem.

This round separates the people who check boxes from the people who understand carbon markets.

**Verdict:** ⚠️ INTEGRITY ISSUES. Two violations: impossible yield + three fraud patterns in the description.

## The data is real

This isn't trivia. Every number on every project card traces to verified sources:

- 10 carbon credit methods, from ARR to DAC to cookstoves
- 7 certification standards (Verra VCS, Gold Standard, CDM, Plan Vivo, Puro.earth, ACR, CAR)
- Prices from Ecosystem Marketplace 2022--2023 and S&P Global Platts
- Yields from IPCC, OECD, and peer-reviewed syntheses
- Fraud patterns from Guardian investigations, Berkeley studies, Carbon Market Watch

Project names and auditor names are fictional. Everything else is sourced.

## For the community manager

You control the pace. Drop when you want. Resolve when you want.

| Command | What happens |
|---------|-------------|
| `/drop` | Post the next project to the group |
| `/resolve` | Close the round, post the verdict |
| `/nextday` | Resolve + drop the next one |
| `/status` | Round state, bet count, player count |

Auto-resolve fires after a configurable delay (default: 60 min after drop). Or turn it off and resolve manually.
