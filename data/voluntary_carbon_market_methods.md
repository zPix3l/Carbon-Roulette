# Voluntary Carbon Market Methods: Prices, Yields, Standards, Geography, and Integrity Risks (2023–2025)

## Overview

This report compiles evidence-based parameters for key voluntary carbon market (VCM) methods across five dimensions: price ranges, sequestration or avoidance yields, certification standards and methodologies, geographic concentration, and documented fraud or over-crediting patterns. All numeric values are tied to explicit sources; values that could not be located in the literature or registry data are marked **UNVERIFIED**.

## Table 3 – Country / Biome Mapping for Each Method

This section lists example countries where each method type has real, registry-listed projects. It is **not** a ranked list by project count (comprehensive ranking would require programmatic registry scraping), but each country is backed by at least one registry or project reference.

### 1. Reforestation / Afforestation (ARR)

Representative countries with registered ARR projects include:

- China – large-scale ARR projects under CDM and Verra, and highlighted as a leading ARR issuer in BeZero’s ARR sector snapshot.[^4]
- Uruguay – significant ARR issuance volumes noted in the same snapshot.[^4]
- United States – multiple ARR/IFM projects across ACR, CAR, and Verra registries.[^11][^12]
- Brazil – ARR and restoration projects in both Verra and Gold Standard registries.[^10][^7]
- India – ARR and agroforestry projects under CDM and Gold Standard.[^8][^10]

### 2. Avoided Deforestation (REDD+)

Countries with numerous REDD+ projects (across Verra and other registries) include:

- Brazil – Amazonian REDD+ projects widely documented and central to Berkeley’s and Guardian investigations.[^17][^18][^56]
- Peru – flagship Amazonian REDD+ projects featured in Guardian/SourceMaterial/Die Zeit investigations.[^56]
- Colombia – multiple REDD+ projects in the Amazon and Chocó regions on Verra’s registry.[^7][^17]
- Democratic Republic of Congo – REDD+ projects in the Congo Basin documented by Climate and Land Use Alliance and registries.[^57]
- Indonesia – large-scale REDD+ projects in peatland and forest areas under Verra and other standards.[^58][^7]

### 3. Mangrove / Blue Carbon Restoration

Blue carbon credits are concentrated in tropical coastal countries:[^21]

- Kenya – community-based mangrove restoration projects issuing blue carbon credits under Plan Vivo and Verra.[^25][^21]
- Indonesia – large mangrove restoration and conservation projects under Verra.[^21][^7]
- Philippines – tidal wetland/seagrass and mangrove restoration projects.[^21]
- Colombia – Caribbean mangrove restoration projects.[^21]
- Madagascar – mangrove restoration under Plan Vivo and Verra.[^25][^21]

### 4. Improved Cookstoves

Improved cookstove projects are widely distributed across the Global South; examples include:[^31][^59]

- Malawi – Gold Standard "Improved Cookstoves for Healthy Families" distributing approximately 70,000 stoves.[^28]
- Peru – Andean community cookstove projects under Gold Standard.[^31]
- Kenya – multiple Verra and Gold Standard cookstove projects.[^7][^31]
- Nepal – cookstove programmes under Gold Standard and CDM.[^8][^31]
- Papua New Guinea – Verra VCS4416 cookstoves project (Tasman Environmental Markets).[^27]

### 5. Biochar

Puro.earth registry data show biochar projects in:[^40]

- United States – several industrial biochar projects (e.g., American BioCarbon, Applied Carbon).[^40]
- Brazil – Aperam BioEnergia biochar project.[^40]
- United Kingdom – multiple Black Bull Biochar projects.[^40]
- France – Carbonapp biochar/waste-wood projects.[^40]
- Philippines – Alcom Carbon Markets biochar projects.[^40]
- Serbia and Norway – additional Puro-registered biochar projects.[^40]

### 6. Direct Air Capture

DACCS projects are still few but include:

- Iceland – Climeworks Orca and Mammoth plants, with offtake contracts backed by buyers like Microsoft and Stripe; associated CDR credits referenced by CDR.fyi.[^42][^43]
- United States – Frontier portfolio includes DAC projects from several U.S.-based companies (e.g., Airhive, CarbonCapture Inc.), with disclosed prices in the 320–580 USD/tCO₂ range.[^42]

### 7. Soil Carbon Sequestration

Agricultural soil carbon projects appear in:

- United States – ACR Soil Enrichment Protocol projects across multiple states.[^47]
- Australia – national soil carbon programmes and credits under the ACCU scheme (outside the main VCM but relevant for method geography).[^60]
- Kenya and other East African countries – Plan Vivo SHAMBA agroforestry and soil carbon projects.[^14]
